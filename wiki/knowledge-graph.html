Finding & seeing

Knowledge Graph

A living map of everything you’ve written — every person, project, date, file and note, connected. You never build it; it builds itself while you write.

It builds itself

Every time you save or leave a note, Nomima indexes just that note: its tags, dates, tasks, files and links become entities and connections in the graph. There is no “sync” or “generate” button — by the time you open the graph, it already reflects your latest edit. Entities mentioned in the same block get connected, and attached files and RSS articles contribute entities from their text too.

Reading the graph

  • Nodes are entities — glowing orbs colored by type, using the exact same colors as the pills in your notes (including your custom types). The legend in the corner lists every type present with its count.
  • Size means connectedness — the more an entity appears across your vault, the bigger its node.
  • Grey means done — completed items dim, so the bright part of your graph is the live part.
  • Edges are relationships — curved lines with animated particles flowing along the connections of whatever you’ve selected.

Navigating

The entity panel

Clicking a node opens a panel showing the entity’s name, type and connection count, plus its relationships grouped by kind — Appears in, Assigned to, Due, Related and so on. Every related item is clickable: hop from a person to a project to the note where they intersect. This is the same entity card you get by clicking a pill in the editor — including its custom attributes (see Mentions & hashtags).

The in-note cover graph

Each notebook can show a compact graph above the page — just that note’s entities and their immediate connections. It’s fully interactive (drag to pan, drag nodes, click to inspect, ⌘+scroll to zoom), and when you scroll down to write, it parks itself to keep the editor fast.

If something looks off

Settings → Vault → Re-index rebuilds the entire graph from scratch, deterministically. It’s the only manual maintenance action in Nomima and exists as a safety net — day to day, the graph keeps itself current.

Privacy: the graph is computed entirely on your Mac. Nothing about your notes — entities, relationships, connections — ever leaves your device. See Settings, privacy & Pro.
