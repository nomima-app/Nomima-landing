Getting started

From download to your first connected note. Nomima runs entirely on your Mac — no account, no cloud, no setup wizard.

Install

  1. Download the .dmg from the Nomima site. Nomima is free and built for Apple Silicon Macs (M1 and later, macOS 11+).
  2. Open the .dmg and drag Nomima into your Applications folder.
  3. Launch it. That’s the whole setup — there is no sign-up, login, or onboarding form.

Where your notes live: on first launch Nomima creates a local vault on your Mac. Everything — notes, files, the knowledge graph — is stored there and never leaves your device. See Settings, privacy & Pro for details.

The Welcome notebook

A fresh vault opens with a “Welcome to Nomima” notebook. It’s a live playground, not a read-only tour: it already contains real @mentions, #hashtags, tasks and dates, so the Knowledge Graph and the colored minimap on the right edge light up immediately. Edit it, break it, delete it — it’s yours.

Write your first note

  1. Create a notebook. Click the new-notebook button in the sidebar, type a title, and press Enter — the caret drops straight into the page.
  2. Just write. Plain paragraphs are fine. Nomima’s structure is opt-in, not required.
  3. Add a task list. Type [] followed by a space at the start of a line, or type /task and pick Task List. Give it a title, press Enter, and start adding items.
  4. Tag a person. Type @ and a name — e.g. @Sara — and press Enter or Tab to accept. It becomes a @Sara pill connected to everything else mentioning Sara.
  5. Tag a project. Type # and a topic — e.g. #launch → #launch.
  6. Type a date in plain words. Write “call the venue tomorrow 3pm” — the date turns into a live tomorrow 3pm pill that shows up in your Home agenda and Dates view automatically.

That’s the core loop: write naturally, tag as you go, and Nomima organizes everything for you — no folders to maintain, no databases to configure.

Find anything

Press ⌘K to open the command palette. It searches notebooks, blocks, every mention and hashtag, dates (“tomorrow”, “friday”…), and files — and it runs commands like creating a notebook or opening Settings. Press Enter on a tag to open its Smart View lens; you can even stack several tags to filter by all or any of them.
