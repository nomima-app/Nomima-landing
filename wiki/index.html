User Guide

Everything Nomima can do, explained

Nomima is a private, offline notebook that turns plain writing into structured knowledge — tasks, people, projects, dates and reminders, all connected in a living graph. This guide covers every feature in detail.

New here? Start with Getting started — it walks you through your first notebook in about five minutes. Otherwise, jump straight to any topic:

Getting started

Install the app, meet the interface, and write your first note.

Blocks

Every block in the “/” menu — checklists, callouts, code, diagrams, Kanban, Gantt and more.

Mentions & hashtags

@people and #projects: how smart tags work, their types, and adding your own custom types with attributes.

Dates & reminders

Type “tomorrow 3pm” and Nomima tracks it. Date pills, countdown reminders and desktop alerts.

Complete & incomplete

One completion system for everything — check items off from anywhere and every view stays in sync.

Smart Views & Home

Lenses that reorganize your whole vault by person, project, date, file or task — plus your daily Home agenda.

Knowledge Graph

The 3D map of everything you’ve written, built automatically as you type.

AI models

Connect Claude, GPT, Gemini, or a fully local model — your keys, your choice.

Summon

The built-in assistant that searches your vault, answers questions and writes into your notes.

MCP server

Let external tools like Claude Code or Cursor read and write your vault — locally and securely.

Publishing

Put a note on the web — pixel-faithful, view-code protected, and always up to date.

Paste, templates & shortcuts

SmartCopy paste, reusable templates, keyboard navigation and other power tools.

Settings & privacy

A map of every setting, exactly what stays on your device, and how it is all free.

How this guide works

The guide is versioned with the app — the footer of every page shows which Nomima release it was written for, and it is updated as part of every release. If something on your screen doesn’t match the guide, check that you’re on the latest version (Settings → About → Check for updates), then tell us.
