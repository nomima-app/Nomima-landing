Dates & reminders

Write dates the way you’d say them. Nomima turns them into live pills, tracks them across your whole vault, and can ping you when the moment arrives.

Date pills

Type a date or time in plain words and, the moment you follow it with a space, comma, period or Enter, it becomes a date pill. Recognized phrases include:

You typeNomima understands
tomorrow, yesterday, next MondayRelative days
June 5, Jun 5 3pmExplicit dates, with or without a time
in 3 days, in 2 hours, in 30 minRelative offsets from now
3pm, 14:00, at 3 p.m.Times today
eod, end of dayToday at 6pm
after tomorrowThe day after tomorrow
  • Your words are kept. The pill shows what you wrote (“tomorrow”, “in 3 hours”) — Nomima stores the resolved moment behind it.
  • Labels stay alive. Same-day pills count down (“in 15 min”) and update as time passes; day-level pills roll over at midnight (“tomorrow” becomes “today”).
  • Overdue turns red. A past date on an unfinished block becomes overdue — and an inline “done?” shortcut appears so you can close it on the spot.
  • Done turns grey. Once the block is complete, its dates show as done and stop nagging you.

Where dates show up

Every date pill is tracked vault-wide, automatically:

  • Home — your daily agenda buckets everything into Overdue, Today, the next few days, and Later.
  • Dates lens in Smart View — a full day-by-day agenda merging dated tasks and dates mentioned in prose, deduplicated, each with a completion checkbox.
  • Smart Scrollbar — dated blocks get a calendar icon on the note’s minimap; overdue ones are red with a count.
  • Notifications — overdue dated tasks appear in the Alerts view and the sidebar bell badge.

Reminders

A reminder is a block with a countdown and a desktop alert. Insert one with /reminder.

Setting one up

  1. Type what it’s about in the label — e.g. “call mom tomorrow 3pm”. The label is normal rich text: mentions, hashtags and date pills all work inside it.
  2. Make sure the label contains a date pill — that’s the due time.
  3. Press Enter to arm it. The countdown starts and the label locks so you can’t nudge it by accident.

Reminder states

StateWhat you see
IdleNo due time yet — the label is fully editable and shows a “Remind me to…” placeholder.
RunningA live countdown ticking down to the due moment.
OverdueRed, showing how long ago it fired (“overdue by 2h”). It stays visible until you act — reminders never silently complete themselves.
DoneGreys out and recedes — struck through, with #complete stamped on the block. Delete the tag to make it active again.
CancelledGreyed out — dismissed without completing. It never counts as overdue.

Controls

  • Clear / Done — marks the reminder complete.
  • Snooze — quick presets: 30 minutes, 1 hour, 3 hours, or 1 day from now. Snoozing updates the reminder’s time and the alert fires again.
  • Reopening — delete the #complete tag on a done reminder and it’s live again.

Desktop notifications

Reminders fire real macOS notifications — and they work even when the note is closed. Each alert fires exactly once per due time and carries Clear and Snooze 1h buttons right on the notification; clicking it opens the note and scrolls to the exact reminder. Overdue reminders also badge the Dock icon until you complete or snooze them.

The bell & the Alerts view

  • The bell in the sidebar shows a red badge with the count of overdue reminders and tasks, or a subtle dot when only upcoming items exist.
  • Opening it (or the Alerts lens in Smart View) shows everything in two sections — Overdue and Upcoming — each row with a one-click ✓ complete, a snooze (for overdue reminders) or dismiss (for upcoming ones), and a link to the source note.

Reference-only dates: a date that’s just context (“we met last Tuesday”) can be marked reference-only — it keeps its pill but stays out of your agendas and never turns red.

Date pill vs. reminder — which one? Use a plain date pill when you want something tracked (it shows in agendas and turns red when missed). Use a reminder when you want to be interrupted at that moment with a notification.
