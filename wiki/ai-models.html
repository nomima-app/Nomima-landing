AI models

Nomima doesn’t lock you into anyone’s AI. Bring your own key for Claude, GPT or Gemini — or point it at a model running entirely on your Mac and keep everything offline.

Pro feature. The Settings → AI Models panel, Summon and the /generate command all require a Nomima Pro license. Everything else in Nomima — including the Knowledge Graph, Smart Views and search — works without any AI model at all.

Want 100% offline AI? Install Ollama or LM Studio, pull a model, and add it as the Local provider. Summon then runs without a single byte leaving your Mac.

Setting up a provider

  1. Open Settings → AI Models.
  2. Pick a provider card and paste your API key (or, for Local, the base URL of your server).
  3. Choose a model — pick from the curated defaults or type any model name your provider supports.
  4. Click Test — Nomima makes one small call to confirm the credentials work.
  5. Set your default provider; that’s what Summon and /generate use unless told otherwise.

How your keys are protected

  • Keys are stored in the macOS Keychain, not in a config file. In the UI you’ll only ever see the last few characters.
  • Keys are used for exactly one thing: calling the provider you configured, when you invoke AI. Nomima has no server of its own and never proxies your traffic.
  • Delete a key any time from the same panel.

If you never configure a provider, Nomima simply never makes an AI call. The app is fully functional as a private, offline notebook.

If something fails

Nomima tells you exactly what went wrong rather than failing silently: an invalid key (fix the key), quota exhausted (top up with your provider), rate limited (wait a moment and retry), or a network error (check your connection — or your local server, if you’re running one).
