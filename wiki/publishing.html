Publishing

Put a notebook on the web as a read-only page that looks exactly like it does in your editor — protected by a view-code, and kept up to date automatically as you keep writing.

Pro feature. Publishing requires a Nomima Pro license. Everything else about your vault stays local — only the notes you explicitly publish ever leave your Mac.

Publishing a note

  1. Open the notebook and click its publish badge in the note header.
  2. Nomima gives you two things: a private link at read.nomima.io and a view-code.
  3. Share them however you like — send the link and code separately for extra care, or share the single combined link that fills the code in automatically.

Anyone opening the bare link is asked for the code first; without it, the page shows nothing.

What readers see

  • A pixel-faithful replica of your note — the published page uses the editor's own rendering and your theme, so every callout, Smart-Tag pill, kanban board, table, equation and diagram looks exactly as it does in the app.
  • It's read-only — readers can't edit, and none of your vault's structure travels with it. The raw note data is never published, only the rendered page.
  • The page shows when the note was created and last edited, so readers know how fresh it is.

It stays up to date by itself

  • Edits auto-update the page. Keep writing — changes flow to the published version automatically (briefly debounced, and skipped entirely when nothing actually changed).
  • Offline-friendly. Publish, update or unpublish without a connection and the action waits in a durable queue, sending when you're back online — even across an app restart.
  • The badge tells the truth. The note header shows the live state at all times: Draft → Publishing → Published, plus Update queued or Failed when something is pending or wrong.

How the offline queue retries

When a publish, update or unpublish operation fails because of a network problem or a server error (5xx), the entry stays in the durable outbox and the worker retries with exponential back-off: the first retry fires after 5 seconds, then 10 s, 20 s, 40 s … up to a maximum of 1 hour between attempts. The badge shows Update queued (not Failed) while retries are pending.

Errors that are not retried — a rejected license key (401/403), a malformed request (other 4xx), or a local database error — are definitive failures: the outbox row is dropped immediately and the badge switches to Failed. The publish menu then shows a Try again button that re-enqueues the operation from scratch.

There is no manual "force retry" command. If the badge is stuck on Failed, open the publish menu and click Try again. The app will re-enqueue the operation and begin the normal retry cycle.

If your Pro license is deactivated while an update is still queued, the backend will reject the next attempt with a license error. That turns the entry into a definitive failure (badge: Failed) — the queued payload is discarded and the already-live page is not automatically taken down. You can unpublish manually while the notebook still shows as published locally.

Sharing attached files

Files attached to the notebook are not shared by default. The publish menu lists them with checkboxes — tick the ones you want readers to have and they appear as download chips at the top of the published page. Everything unticked stays on your Mac.

Know your readers

Every published note has a Views panel: total, unique and returning visitors, average read time, a views-over-time chart, top countries, and a per-visitor list showing coarse location (country and city — no raw IP is stored), device, and time spent. Readers can optionally leave a name when they open the page.

Accessing the analytics panel

Analytics are available in two places once a notebook is published:

  • View-count pill in the note header. A small pill showing the total view count appears in the notebook header next to the publish badge. It refreshes automatically every 30 seconds while you are online. Clicking it opens the Views & visitors panel.
  • Link inside the publish menu. Open the publish menu (click the publish badge), scroll to the bottom of the published state, and click View analytics & visitors to open the same panel.

The panel shows total, unique and returning visitor counts, average read time, a 14-day views bar chart, top countries (up to six), and a per-visitor list with coarse location (city and country — no raw IP address is stored), device (OS and browser), time spent, and an optional name the visitor may have entered. Analytics require an internet connection; the panel shows a message rather than stale data when you are offline.

There is no option to disable analytics tracking per note or globally. All published notes collect the same view metrics on the nomima.io backend.

Privacy & control

  • Only people with both the link and the view-code can read the page.
  • Unpublish any time — the page is taken down, including from the offline queue if you're not connected.
  • Publishing is the only Nomima feature that uploads note content anywhere, and it does so only for the notes you choose — see the complete privacy story.
