Privacy: what stays, what leaves

Nomima is local-first in the literal sense. Your vault — every note, file, comment and graph relationship — lives in a folder on your Mac (under ~/Library/Application Support), stored in an ordinary SQLite database. No account exists, no telemetry is collected, nothing syncs anywhere.

The complete list of network calls

CallWhenWhat’s sent
Update checkOn launch / when you click “Check for updates”A request for a version file — nothing about you or your notes
License validationOn activation, then occasionallyYour license key and a machine fingerprint — never note content
AI provider callsOnly when you use Summon or AI blocks, with a provider you configuredThe conversation and relevant note context, to your chosen provider (or to a local model: nothing leaves at all)
Summon web searchOnly if you enabled it in SettingsThe search query
PublishingOnly when you publish, update or unpublish a noteThe note’s rendered page (and files you explicitly ticked) to the read.nomima.io service

That’s the whole list. Offline, with no AI configured, Nomima makes zero network requests that involve your data — and every feature outside the AI section works exactly the same.

Activating a license

  1. Open Settings → About and enter your license key.
  2. Activation registers this Mac to your license (one quick online check; beta keys work fully offline).
  3. After that, Nomima trusts the cached license — losing internet never locks you out of Pro features.

Stored safely: the key lives in your macOS Keychain. Deactivate from the same panel if you’re moving to a new machine.

Vault care

  • Back up your vault folder like any other documents — it’s self-contained and portable.
  • Re-index (Settings → Vault) rebuilds search and the Knowledge Graph from your notes if anything ever looks stale.
  • Storage stats on the same panel show exactly where the space goes.
